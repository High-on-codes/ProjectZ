Part I

Privacy Policy

Effective July 1, 2026. This policy explains what Project Z does and does not do with your data, in plain terms first and precise terms second.

1

Overview

Project Z is built as a local-first application. Our design goal is simple: the content of your goals and courses should never need to leave your device to be useful. This policy covers the Project Z iOS app, this website, and our beta waitlist form.

In short: we do not operate a server that stores your goals, your courses, or your usage activity. The only personal information we ever hold is the email address you choose to give us for the beta waitlist.
2

Information we collect

We collect the minimum needed to run a waitlist and, later, to process optional subscription payments:

DataWhenPurposeWhere it's stored
Email addressYou submit our intake formSend your beta inviteLocal Database (secured processor)

We never ask for your name, contact list, location, photos, or device identifiers, and the app does not request any permission it doesn't need to function.

3

Information we do not collect

  • The goals, prompts, or courses you generate inside the app
  • Your usage patterns, session length, or feature interactions
  • Advertising identifiers or any data used for ad targeting
  • Precise or approximate location
  • Contacts, photos, or other device content

We do not sell, rent, monetize, or share this category of data — because we never have access to it in the first place. There is no analytics SDK, no ad network SDK, and no third-party crash reporter bundled into the app.

4

How on-device processing works

When you type a goal, Project Z runs inference locally using Apple's on-device intelligence frameworks. No network socket is opened during generation. The resulting course is written to a sandboxed SwiftData store that is unique to your installation of the app and is included in your device's standard encrypted backups (iCloud or local), governed by Apple's own backup encryption — not by us.

Because generation happens on-device, we have no visibility into what you type, what courses are generated, or how you use them. We cannot recover this data for you if it's lost, since we never had a copy.

4.1

Technical Incapacity to Monitor Content

Because our architecture leaves zero network sockets open during execution and writes exclusively to an isolated local sandbox, it is a technical impossibility for Project Z's creators, developers, or owners to view, log, check, filter, or intercept your inputs. We maintain absolutely no administrative access or command visibility over what is typed, searched, or generated within your local application sandbox.

5

Third-party services & data sharing

We rely on a small number of third parties strictly for functions we don't build ourselves:

  • Processing Engine (Formspree) — collects waitlist email submissions securely from our intake form. We retain waitlist emails only as long as needed to send your invite, and we remove entries from the database on request.
  • Apple, Inc. — processes App Store purchases, subscription billing, and (if you opt in) crash diagnostics, under Apple's own privacy terms. We receive only an anonymized, aggregated purchase confirmation — never your card details.

We do not use any advertising network, analytics platform, or data broker. We have never sold personal information and we do not intend to.

6

Data retention & deletion

Waitlist email addresses are retained until your invite is sent and for up to 12 months afterward for support purposes, unless you ask us to delete it sooner. Course and goal data never reaches us, so there is nothing on our servers to delete — deleting the app from your device, or deleting a course inside it, removes that data completely and immediately, including from any iCloud backup on your next sync.

To request early deletion of your waitlist email, contact us using the details in Contact Us.

7

Your rights (GDPR, UK GDPR, and CCPA/CPRA)

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal data we hold about you (in practice, this is limited to your waitlist email and purchase confirmation, if any)
  • Request correction or deletion of that data
  • Object to or restrict our processing of it
  • Request a portable copy of it
  • Withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the app's core functionality
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority

Because Project Z does not sell personal information and does not use your data for cross-context behavioral advertising, there is no "opt out of sale" flow to run — there is nothing being sold. Residents may still submit a rights request as described in Contact Us, and we will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law.

8

International data transfers

Waitlist emails processed may be transferred to and stored securely on infrastructure using standard contractual clauses and safeguards for cross-border processing. Because your goal and course content never leaves your device, no international transfer of that content ever occurs.

9

Children's privacy

Project Z is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because our waitlist only asks for an email address and the app itself collects no personal data at all, the practical exposure is minimal — but if you believe a child has submitted an email to our waitlist, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10

Security measures

Waitlist submissions are encrypted in transit via TLS. On-device data is protected by iOS's sandboxing and file-system encryption. Because we do not operate a central database of user content, there is no central store of your course data for anyone to breach. No transmission or storage system is completely immune to risk, and we cannot guarantee absolute security, but our architecture is deliberately designed to minimize what could ever be exposed.

11

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy in a way that materially affects your rights, we will update the "Last revised" date above and, where required by law, notify you through the app or the email on file. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
